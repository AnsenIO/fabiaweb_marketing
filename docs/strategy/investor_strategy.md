# FABIABox Investor Strategy

> **Goal:** close a €500k–€1M Pre-Seed SAFE by converting proven traction into investor conviction.  
> **Trigger:** activate only after the traction gates in [`master_strategy.md`](master_strategy.md) are met.

---

## 1. When to activate

Activate the investor track when **at least two** of the following traction gates are true:

| Gate | Rationale |
|------|-----------|
| ≥ 50 paying Agentic plan customers **or** ≥ €2.5k MRR | Proof of willingness to pay |
| ≥ 50 hardware reservations (€500 each) = €25k committed | Proof of high-ticket demand |
| ≥ 1,000 qualified waitlist subscribers with product-interest tags | Future pipeline visibility |
| Repeatable organic funnel with known shop-visit-to-purchase conversion | Predictable growth |

## 2. Target investor profile

| Tier | Profile | Typical cheque | Role in round |
|------|---------|----------------|---------------|
| **European angels** | Exited founders, operators, AI practitioners | €10k–€50k | First cheques, social proof |
| **Angel syndicates** | UK/EU syndicate leads (e.g. Odin, Roundtable) | €50k–€150k | Concentrated demand |
| **Pre-seed / seed micro-VCs** | €20–€60M funds, B2B/infra focus | €100k–€300k | Lead or co-lead |
| **Corporate / strategic** | NVIDIA, AMD, cloud/hardware ecosystem funds | €50k–€200k | Validation, partnerships |
| **Customer-angels** | Technical founders/agencies using FABIABox | €10k–€25k | Aligned users with capital |

**Geography:** Europe-first (France, UK, Germany, Nordics) to match EUR positioning and time zones.  
**Stage:** Pre-Seed / Seed, comfortable with hardware + agentic services risk.

## 3. Investment narrative

### One-liner

> FABIABox is the AI co-founder that ships companies — and the sovereign hardware stack they run on.

### Story arc

1. **Market shift:** AI agents are moving from copilots to autonomous executors. Non-technical founders need a co-founder, not another SaaS tool.
2. **Problem:** Building a tech company still requires a technical co-founder or an expensive agency; cloud AI is expensive and locked-in.
3. **Solution:** FABIABox combines an agentic build/operate pipeline with sovereign AI hardware, so founders can own their stack end-to-end.
4. **Traction proof:** [insert live metrics] paying customers, hardware reservations, waitlist, organic funnel.
5. **Ambition:** become the default infrastructure for the agentic-company era, from solo founders to enterprise innovation labs.

### Why now

- Agentic AI capabilities crossed a reliability threshold in 2025–2026.
- Demand for sovereign/on-premise AI is rising (regulation, latency, cost).
- Hardware supply chains for edge AI workstations are opening up.
- Non-technical founders are the largest underserved segment in the AI boom.

## 4. Proof points

Investors will look for evidence in three buckets:

| Bucket | Proof |
|--------|-------|
| **Demand** | Waitlist size, MRR, paying customers, hardware reservations, conversion rates |
| **Execution** | Ships delivered, time-to-live-URL, customer testimonials, demo videos |
| **Defensibility** | Sovereign hardware stack, agent pipeline IP, customer data, community moat |

## 5. Channels & tactics

### Warm intros (P0)

- Map every existing contact who can introduce to angels/VCs.
- Use traction updates as the excuse to re-engage dormant relationships.
- Ask customers and advisors for intros.

### LinkedIn (P0)

- Founder profile posts weekly “traction receipts.”
- Direct, personalised outreach to target investors with a concise pitch + metrics.
- Comment on investor posts to build familiarity before the ask.

### Investor newsletters / syndicates (P1)

- Submit deck to relevant European deal-flow newsletters.
- Run a syndicate via Odin, Roundtable, or AngelList (EU compliant).

### Demo days / pitch events (P1)

- Apply to AI/hardware focused accelerators and demo days.
- Use live customer demos instead of slides where possible.

### Data room (P0)

- Keep lightweight but complete: pitch deck, financial model, cap table, traction dashboard, product demo, team bios, legal docs.
- Update monthly with new metrics.

## 6. Materials

| Material | Purpose | Owner |
|----------|---------|-------|
| **One-pager** | First share after intro | Founder |
| **Pitch deck (10–12 slides)** | Meeting narrative | Founder + design agent |
| **Financial model** | Unit economics, runway, hiring plan | Founder + finance agent |
| **Traction dashboard** | Live metrics screenshot | Analytics agent |
| **Demo video** | Product walkthrough | Product agent |
| **Data room** | Due diligence | Founder |

### Suggested deck flow

1. Hook: “The AI Co-Founder That Ships Your Company.”
2. Problem: non-technical founders can’t ship; cloud AI is rented and expensive.
3. Solution: FABIABox agentic pipeline + sovereign hardware.
4. Product: Build Plan, Operate Plan, hardware tiers.
5. Traction: MRR, reservations, waitlist, case studies.
6. Market: TAM/SAM/SOM for agentic company creation + sovereign AI hardware.
7. Business model: subscriptions + hardware margins + enterprise services.
8. Go-to-market: traction strategy summary.
9. Competitive landscape: agencies, no-code tools, cloud providers, DGX.
10. Team / roadmap.
11. Ask: €500k–€1M Pre-Seed SAFE, use of funds.
12. Appendix: benchmarks, tech stack, risks.

## 10. Risks & mitigations

| Risk | Mitigation |
|------|------------|
| Traction gates take longer than expected | Keep burn low; continue organic growth before fundraising |

| EUR-only positioning limits US investors | Frame as European advantage; remain open to US co-investors |
