## 1. Digital Co-Founders: Transforming Imagination into Viable Solo Business via Agentic AI

**Source:** `digital-co-founders-agentic-ai.pdf`  
**arXiv ID:** 2511.09533v1  
**Relevance:** High — directly about AI agents as co-founders for solo entrepreneurs.

### Key findings

Solo founders face a specific tension:
- They have freedom and autonomy.
- They are constrained by **limited human resources, cognitive bandwidth, and emotional resilience**.
- They must act as strategist, creator, marketer, operator, and financial manager at the same time.

AI agents can absorb routine tasks (prototyping, content, customer interaction, data analysis), but they also introduce new demands:
- prompt design,
- system oversight,
- strategic orchestration of human–AI collaboration.

### Messaging implications

| Insight | Copy angle |
|---|---|
| Solo founders suffer **decision fatigue** and **cognitive overload**. | Position Fabia as the single teammate that decides and executes, so the founder stops context-switching. |
| Founders experience **fear of visibility**, **impostor syndrome**, and **risk aversion**. | Human-side angle: "Built for non-technical humans" — reduces shame, signals safety and patience. |
| Founders care about **identity and authenticity**. | Emphasize that the founder stays in control; Fabia is a partner, not a replacement. |
| Imagination must be turned into **tested, repeatable value**. | Functional angle: "From docs to MVP to revenue" — concrete pipeline. |
| AI is a **cognitive amplifier**, interpretation remains human. | Avoid claiming Fabia replaces the founder; frame it as amplification. |

## 2. Live Meta campaign learnings (Day 1)

## 3. Messaging strategy for non-technical founders

### Core tension

Non-technical founders want to build a company but feel blocked by:
- Not knowing how to code.
- Not having a technical co-founder.
- Fear of being judged by developers or investors.
- Overwhelm from tools, agencies, and freelancers.

### Two tested frames

#### A. Functional / non-human frame
Focus on what Fabia does, not how it feels.

> **Headline:** No tech co-founder? No problem.  
> **Sub:** Fabia reads your docs, builds your MVP, and runs your go-to-market stack — locally from your desk.  
> **CTA:** Start building →

- Uses direct problem/solution structure.
- Avoids emotional language.
- Leans on efficiency and capability.

#### B. Human / emotional frame
Focus on safety, identity, and partnership.

> **Headline:** Your AI co-founder for non-technical humans.  
> **Sub:** You bring the idea. Fabia brings the team. No code, no jargon, no shame — just a shipped company you still control.  
> **CTA:** Meet your co-founder →

- Names the audience explicitly ("non-technical humans").
- Addresses impostor syndrome and fear of judgment.
- Positions Fabia as a patient, non-judgmental partner.

### Why test both

The literature shows that solo founders are driven by **both** practical constraints (limited resources, skills) **and** emotional needs (safety, identity, control). Meta data shows the current functional angles (V5/V6) win for this audience, but we have not explicitly tested an emotional, human-centric frame. Testing both lets us learn whether non-technical founders respond better to capability claims or to reassurance.
